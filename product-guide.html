01

What this tool is

A single, network-wide view of every Catalyst currently moving through EduSpots' 6-step induction pathway — and where each one has stalled.

New Catalysts join through a defined pathway — part of the "Ignite" phase, ahead of "Catalyse" and "Sustain" — that takes them through community engagement, team building, safeguarding training, systems/app training, digital skills, and pedagogical training. Right now, tracking where 130+ Catalysts sit in that pathway likely lives in a patchwork of spreadsheets and individual RC memory. That works when a handful of new Catalysts join each month. It starts to break down when you need to answer: who's been stuck on Step 2 for three months? Which RC's induction pipeline is moving fastest? Is anyone about to fall through the cracks before they ever run a session for a Spot?

This tracker consolidates that picture. It doesn't replace the RC relationships that actually move a Catalyst through induction — it sits above them, giving the Head of Programme and Product Operations a network-level view that makes onboarding bottlenecks visible before they become a Catalyst quietly drifting away.

Every Catalyst, one view
See all 136 Catalysts and exactly where each one sits in the 6-step pathway without opening a spreadsheet or messaging an RC for an update.
Specific, actionable flags
Every flagged Catalyst carries the exact reason — "No step progress in 34 days," not just a red dot — so you know what conversation to have before you open the detail.
A pipeline, not a list
The Kanban-style board shows induction as what it actually is — a pathway with stages — so bottlenecks at a specific step are visible at a glance.
Human decision, always
The tool surfaces who needs a nudge. Every outreach, rescheduled training, and graduation sign-off is made by a person — the RC, the Head of Ops, or both together.

02

The core principle

Automation flags and summarizes. A human — always the RC — decides.

🌿
Why this matters for induction specifically

A tool that auto-escalated "stalled" Catalysts or auto-graduated anyone who ticked all six boxes would remove exactly the judgment that makes onboarding work. An RC knows that a Catalyst's safeguarding training got rescheduled because of a family emergency, that someone finished all six steps on paper but clearly isn't ready to lead a session yet, that a "stalled" flag on paper is really just a Catalyst waiting on a training date the RC already has scheduled. The tracker doesn't have that context. So it flags; you decide.

This is stated as plainly as possible, because it's the single most important design decision in the tool: whether a Catalyst is actually ready to graduate induction is never an automated decision. "Graduated" status means the 6-step checklist is complete — nothing more. It is a prompt for an RC conversation and sign-off, not a substitute for it. The "Acknowledge for follow-up" button on a stalled Catalyst doesn't change their step, reschedule a training, or mark anything complete — it records that a human has seen the flag and taken ownership of the follow-up.

03

Overview view

The starting screen. A pulse-check on the whole induction pipeline before you go deeper.

Overview
KPI row · step-by-step funnel chart · RC cluster distribution chart

The Overview loads first when you open the tracker. It answers the most common Monday-morning question: is the induction pipeline healthy right now, or is something backing up?

The KPI row gives five numbers at a glance. The two charts underneath show exactly where the pipeline is bottlenecked and whether any RC cluster has a disproportionate share of new Catalysts to manage.

  • In induction Every Catalyst who hasn't yet completed all 6 steps. This is the working population the tool exists to track.
  • Graduated Catalysts who've completed all 6 steps. Shown as a headline figure, but see the note in Section 08 — completing the checklist isn't the same as an RC confirming readiness.
  • Needs RC follow-up The count of Catalysts currently flagged watch or stalled. This is the number to track week-on-week: going up means the pipeline is backing up; going down means follow-ups are working.
  • Avg. days to graduate For Catalysts who've completed induction — how long it took from their start date to their final step. Useful for setting realistic expectations with new RCs or funders about onboarding timelines.
  • RC clusters A simple count of the four Regional Coordinator clusters currently managing induction — confirms the network context at a glance.
  • Catalysts by induction step A horizontal bar chart showing how many Catalysts currently sit at each of the 6 steps, plus Graduated. A tall bar at an early step with a much shorter one after it is the clearest possible signal of a bottleneck — e.g. lots of Catalysts stuck before Safeguarding, worth investigating whether training sessions are being scheduled often enough.
  • Catalysts by RC cluster A doughnut chart comparing how many Catalysts each of the four RCs is currently managing through induction. An obvious imbalance is a prompt for a conversation about workload — not a performance judgment on any individual RC.

04

Pipeline board

A Kanban-style board — one column per induction step. The fastest way to see where the pipeline is thin or backed up.

Pipeline board
7 columns (6 steps + Graduated) · filterable by RC cluster · click a card for detail

The board gives you a spatial read of the whole pipeline. Every Catalyst appears as a card in the column matching their current step, colour-coded by status — green for on-track, gold for watch, clay/red for stalled, sky blue once graduated. A thin column early in the pathway with a thick one right after it means Catalysts are moving through that step quickly; the reverse means something is holding people back.

Filter by RC cluster before a check-in call — you'll see exactly which of that RC's Catalysts are where in the pipeline before you dial in, rather than asking them to talk you through it from memory.

Clicking any card opens the same Catalyst detail modal described in Section 07 — full checklist, dates, and (if flagged) the specific reason.

  • Card colour Green border = on-track. Gold border = watch. Clay/red border = stalled. Sky blue border = graduated. The colour encodes the computed status — no manual tagging required.
  • Card content Each card shows the Catalyst's name, their assigned Spot, and their RC — enough to know who they are and who owns their follow-up without opening the detail.
  • RC cluster filter Narrows the whole board to a single RC's Catalysts — the fastest way to prep for a cluster-level check-in.
  • Column counts Each column header shows how many Catalysts currently sit at that step, so you don't have to count cards to spot a bottleneck.
  • Graduated column The 7th column. A Catalyst appearing here means their checklist is complete — see Section 08 for what that status does and doesn't mean.

05

RC clusters

Induction health grouped by Regional Coordinator — the fastest way to identify which cluster needs the most support this cycle.

RC clusters
4 clusters · on-track / watch / stalled / graduated counts · avg. days to graduate per cluster

Each row represents one Regional Coordinator and the Catalysts currently in their induction pipeline. This is the view to open before a cluster-level reporting conversation — with the Head of Education, a funder, or in a trustee update on Catalyst growth.

An obvious gap between clusters — one RC with a much higher stalled count, or a much longer average days-to-graduate — is a signal, not a conclusion. It's a prompt for a direct conversation about what that RC needs from HQ: more training session slots, admin support, or just a heads-up that a few Catalysts need a nudge.

  • Total Total number of Catalysts currently assigned to this RC's cluster, across all stages of induction.
  • On-track / Watch / Stalled A breakdown of this cluster's Catalysts by current status, using the same three-tier language as the rest of the tracker.
  • Graduated How many of this RC's Catalysts have completed all 6 steps.
  • Avg. days to graduate The average time this RC's graduated Catalysts took to complete induction — useful for spotting whether one cluster's pathway is consistently slower than the others.

06

Stalled Catalysts

The action view — every Catalyst flagged watch or stalled, with the specific reason and a way to take ownership of the follow-up.

Stalled Catalysts
Flagged Catalysts only · specific reasons · acknowledge for follow-up · filterable by RC

This view collects every Catalyst who has crossed a watch or stalled threshold — sorted with the more serious cases (stalled) ahead of the early warnings (watch). Each card states the specific reason in plain language, so you arrive at a follow-up conversation already knowing what you're dealing with.

The "Acknowledge for follow-up" button records that someone — an RC or the Head of Ops — has taken ownership of reviewing that Catalyst's situation. It doesn't advance their step, doesn't mark anything complete, and doesn't send a notification. It's a human handshake: I've seen this, I'm on it.

  • Specific flagged reason Every card states the exact date-derived condition that triggered the flag, e.g. "No step progress in 34 days" or "Still on Safeguarding 96 days since induction start." Never just a colour.
  • Spot and RC on the card The Catalyst's assigned Spot and RC are shown directly on the card — you don't need to open a detail panel to know who to contact about follow-up.
  • Acknowledge for follow-up Marks a Catalyst as having been reviewed. The button becomes a confirmation state and the card dims. In this prototype, acknowledgements are stored in the browser and persist across refreshes on the same device; in a live system they'd be stored centrally with a timestamp and the name of whoever acknowledged it.
  • RC cluster filter Narrows the list to a single RC's flagged Catalysts — useful for preparing a focused follow-up list before a call.
  • Sort order Stalled Catalysts always appear above watch Catalysts, so the most urgent cases are at the top without any manual sorting.
💡
How to use this view in a weekly ops review

Open this view at the start of each week. Work through the stalled cards first. For each: does the reason match something you already know — a rescheduled training, a Catalyst on a planned break? Acknowledge it. Is it a surprise? It needs a message to the relevant RC. Then move to watch cards — the early warnings worth a light-touch check-in before they tip into stalled.

07

Catalyst detail

The full record for one Catalyst — reached by clicking any card on the board or any name in the Stalled Catalysts view.

Catalyst detail (modal)
Full 6-step checklist · dates · "what's next" · flagged reason if applicable

This is the closest the tracker gets to a case file, and it's deliberately narrow in scope. It shows: name, assigned Spot, assigned RC, induction start date, days since last step progress, the complete 6-step checklist with completion dates, and — if flagged — the specific reason stated plainly at the top.

Beneath the checklist, an auto-generated "what's next" line names the next incomplete step and who to contact to schedule it. This is a convenience, not a decision — it's derived directly from the checklist and the assigned RC, nothing more.

  • Induction checklist All 6 steps listed in order. Completed steps show a checkmark and completion date. The current step is highlighted. Remaining steps show as empty.
  • Days since last progress The exact number of days since the Catalyst's most recent completed step — the same figure that drives the watch/stalled status rules, shown transparently rather than hidden behind a badge.
  • "What's next" line Names the next incomplete step and suggests reaching out to the assigned RC to schedule it. For a graduated Catalyst, this instead notes that RC sign-off is still needed to confirm readiness.
  • Flagged reason (if applicable) Shown at the top of the modal in the same plain language used everywhere else in the tool — no separate explanation needed to understand why this Catalyst needs attention.

08

How status is calculated

Every status is computed by a named function with explicit, visible rules — no hidden scoring, no black box.

The rules are the same regardless of which view you're in. A Catalyst flagged "stalled" on the board is flagged for the same reason they appear in the Stalled Catalysts view. The reason is always shown alongside the status — never implied by a colour alone.

Catalyst induction status

Stalled
Either of the following:
No step progress in 30+ days, OR still on the current step 90+ days after induction start.
Watch
Either of the following (and not already stalled):
No step progress in 14–29 days, OR still on the current step 45–89 days after induction start.
On-track
Neither of the above conditions apply.
Recent step progress and a reasonable pace relative to induction start. No flag is raised.
Graduated
All 6 steps complete.
The checklist is done — this is a data state, not a certification. See the note below.
🔒
What "graduated" can and cannot mean

Graduated means the 6-step checklist is complete — nothing more. It does not mean an RC has confirmed the Catalyst is ready to independently run sessions at a Spot. That judgment call is always human, and it happens outside this tool, through the RC relationship. The tracker's job stops at telling you the checklist is done; whether that Catalyst is actually ready is exactly the kind of context a spreadsheet of dates can never capture.

📋
The thresholds can be adjusted

The 30-day, 90-day, 14-day, and 45-day thresholds above are starting points, not fixed policy. In a live deployment, the Head of Ops and RCs would review these together and set thresholds that reflect what EduSpots considers a healthy induction pace — which may reasonably differ by region, given differing training session availability.

09

Typical weekly workflow

How the Head of Programme and Product Operations would use this in practice — from Monday morning to a follow-up conversation with an RC.

1
Open Overview — read the pipeline pulse
Check the KPI row. Has the "needs RC follow-up" count gone up or down since last week? Is average days-to-graduate trending longer? Does the step funnel chart show a new bottleneck at a specific stage? These numbers set the agenda for the rest of the session.
2
Go to Stalled Catalysts — triage the flagged cases
Work through the stalled cards first. For each: is this already known and in hand — a rescheduled training, a planned break? Acknowledge it. Is it a surprise? Note it for a message to the relevant RC. Repeat for watch cards — the early warnings worth a light check-in before they become urgent.
3
Go to Pipeline board — spot where the bottleneck actually is
If the Overview chart flagged a bottleneck at a specific step, filter the board by RC cluster to see whether it's network-wide or concentrated in one region. A bottleneck at Step 3 (Safeguarding) across every cluster might mean training sessions simply aren't scheduled often enough — that's a scheduling fix, not an individual-RC problem.
4
Prepare for RC cluster calls — check RC clusters, then filter the board
Before a check-in call with, say, Abdul Wadud Suleiman (Central/Western Regions), open RC clusters to see his overall pipeline health relative to the others. Then filter the board or the Stalled Catalysts view to his cluster specifically. Arrive at the call with a focused agenda rather than a general "how's induction going?"
5
Make the readiness call — outside the tool
The tracker tells you who's stuck and where. The actual decisions — rescheduling a training block, confirming a graduated Catalyst is genuinely ready to run a session, deciding a stalled Catalyst needs a different kind of support — are made by the Head of Ops and the relevant RC together, using the tracker as the shared evidence base rather than the decision-maker.

10

Who does what

The tracker serves three roles differently. The tool does the date-tracking; people make every decision that matters.

Task Who In the tool
Monitor network-wide induction health Head of Ops Overview KPIs + charts
Identify which Catalysts need urgent follow-up Head of Ops Stalled Catalysts — stalled cards
Spot pipeline bottlenecks at a specific step Head of Ops Overview step funnel chart + Pipeline board
Prepare for RC cluster check-in calls Head of Ops RC RC clusters + board/list filtered by RC
Record that a flagged Catalyst is being followed up RC Head of Ops Acknowledge for follow-up button
Schedule or reschedule an induction training step RC Outside the tool — informed by the "what's next" line
Confirm a graduated Catalyst is ready to run sessions RC Head of Ops Outside the tool — never automated
Report onboarding throughput to funders or trustees Head of Ops Overview KPIs · RC clusters table
Compute induction status and flag stalled Catalysts Automated computeCatalystStatus() in data.js

11

Next steps if built for real

This is a working prototype built on synthetic data. Here's what a live deployment would add.

Live data connection
Connect to EduSpots' actual Catalyst records — likely the same app/data system used for Step 4 (Systems development) training — so the tracker reflects real induction progress without a new data entry habit.
WhatsApp / email digest
A weekly summary sent to the Head of Ops and each RC listing new flags in their cluster — so the tracker pushes urgent information rather than waiting to be opened.
CSV export
Export the Stalled Catalysts list or the RC clusters table to CSV for inclusion in trustee packs, funder reports, or RC performance conversations — without reformatting anything manually.
RC-level authentication
Each RC logs in to see only their own cluster's Catalysts — a more focused daily view, and appropriate given the personal details involved in onboarding a new Catalyst.
Acknowledge with attribution
Persist acknowledgements centrally with a timestamp and the name of the person who logged them — a clear audit trail of who took ownership of a stalled Catalyst's follow-up and when, rather than the current per-browser storage.
Integration with Project #1 and #4
Cross-reference a Catalyst's induction status with their assigned Spot's health data (Project #1) and their ongoing safeguarding training compliance once graduated (Project #4) — so the full Catalyst lifecycle, not just onboarding, is visible in one place.
